About Me

    I grew up the youngest of four, surrounded by noise, animals, and grandparents from every continent, filling my nose with spices and my heart with tales from all over the world, but it didn’t give me much space to think. My parents— avid readers and lovers of stories— gave us books to get lost in stories that spirited our imagination and many questions they didn’t have answers for. Like so many people, I am a product of my upbringing, lending me a wide scope to view the world and a love of people-watching.
    I am a copywriter, editor, server, lousy karaoke singer, and surprisingly decent bachata dancer. I have lived around the country, giving me an open view of people and a wide net of loved ones to crash with when I feel the wanderlust nip at my heels. I write stories about people, how they interact with one another, and how they react to extreme situations. My first novel, LEAVE WHITE MOUNTAIN ALONE, explores the relationships hidden within a tight-knit family and the secrets that can be ignored to keep the status quo. It is a novel about the various ways a person can be impacted by trauma, how it can mold you, and the extremes someone can go to break a cycle.
